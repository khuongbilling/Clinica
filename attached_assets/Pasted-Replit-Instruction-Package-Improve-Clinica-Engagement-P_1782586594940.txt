Replit Instruction Package: Improve Clinica Engagement, Purpose, Progression, and Market Appeal

Project Goal

Clinica should become more than a battle demo. It should clearly feel like:

A fantasy medical RPG where players restore a body kingdom by learning how medicine, nursing, and clinical judgment work.

The current game has useful mechanics, including heroes, battle systems, Patient Stability, Disease Corruption, Codex, items, and clinical reasoning. However, a nonmedical player may still ask:

What is the point of the game?
Why am I fighting this monster?
Why should I care?
What happens after I win?
How does this help me learn medicine or nursing?

This improvement pass should make the purpose, story, rewards, and progression clearer.

Do not rebuild the app. Make focused improvements that build on the existing system.

---

Main Design Direction

The game should communicate this within the first 30 seconds:

Clinica is a kingdom shaped like the human body.

Each region keeps the kingdom alive:
Air helps it breathe.
River carries blood.
Fire fights infection.
Energy fuels the body.
Mind guides decisions.
Protection keeps the body safe.

Disease corruption is spreading.

Your healer team must read clues, stabilize the patient, choose the right response, reduce Disease Corruption, and restore each region.

The player should understand:

Patient Stability = keep the patient alive.
Disease Corruption = the disease’s control over the body.
Care Chain = Scout → Stabilize → Counter → Reassess.
Rewards = stars, Codex Shards, heroes, and kingdom restoration.

Priority 3: Add Compact Objective Strip During Battle

During battle, add a small objective strip that stays visible but does not crowd the screen.

Example:

Goal: Scout → Stabilize → Counter → Reassess

or:

Current Goal: Reveal clue, then use an Air counter.

For nonmedical profile, show clearer guidance:

First, reveal the hidden clue. Clues help you avoid guessing.

The objective strip should update after each major step.

Example progression:

Before scout:
Goal: Reveal the hidden clue.

After scout:
Goal: Stabilize the patient.

After stabilize:
Goal: Use an Air counter to lower Corruption.

After counter:
Goal: Reassess the response.

Priority 4: Improve First Battle Feedback for Nonmedical Players

For the nonmedical learning profile, feedback should be simple and encouraging.

Add or improve action feedback messages.

After Scout

You found a hidden clue. Clues help you avoid guessing.

If Wheezing is revealed:

Hidden clue revealed: Wheezing. Wheezing often means narrowed airways, so Air actions will work better.

After Stabilize

Stability improved. A stable patient gives your team more time to act.

After Counter/Treatment

Disease Corruption dropped. This means the disease process is being controlled.

After Reassess

Good. Treatment is not complete until you check the patient’s response.

After Care Attempt

Care Attempt helped only a little. It slowed the corruption, but did not target the main problem. Try using clues to choose a stronger action.

After Wrong/Poor Fit Skill

This action had limited effect because it does not match the main problem. Look at the clues and system type.

Priority 6: Add Visible Kingdom Restoration Progress

The player needs to feel that winning battles changes the world.

Add simple region progress tracking.

Example player state:

player.regionProgress = player.regionProgress || {
  Air: 0,
  River: 0,
  Fire: 0,
  Energy: 0,
  Protection: 0,
  Mind: 0,
  Storm: 0,
  Forge: 0,
  Filter: 0,
  Growth: 0
};

After winning Air Sprite:

player.regionProgress.Air += 1;

Display:

Air Region: 1/5 Restored

For now, use simple progress numbers. A full animated map can come later.

Before battle, show recommended roles.

Example:

Priority 11: Keep Codex as Deeper Learning, Not Main Tutorial

The Codex should not be the only place that explains the game.

Use this structure:

Tutorial popups = teach the first action.
Mission briefing = explain the current goal.
Battle feedback = explain why actions worked.
Victory screen = summarize what was learned.
Codex = deeper reference.

Add this Codex line if useful:

Tutorials teach the first steps. The Codex explains the deeper clinical meaning.

---

Priority 12: Add Trust and Scope Language

Because this game teaches health and nursing concepts, include a simple disclaimer in settings, Codex, or about screen.

Use:

Clinica is an educational game for learning body systems and clinical reasoning. It is not medical advice and does not replace professional training, clinical judgment, or licensed care.

For medication-like actions, keep wording safe:

as ordered
per protocol
anticipate
support
monitor
reassess

Avoid wording that implies independent prescribing.

Priority 13: Monetization Readiness Without Adding Payment Yet

Do not add payment yet.

But structure the game so future monetization makes sense.

Free demo should include:

Air region intro
First battle tutorial
Starter heroes
One summon
Basic Codex
Air Temple restoration

Future paid content can include:

Additional body systems
Advanced case packs
NCLEX Judgment Tower
Progress analytics
Unlimited practice
Cosmetics

Do not build the payment system now. Focus on making the free demo compelling.

Do Not Do

Testing Checklist

After this improvement pass, test as a nonmedical user.

The user should be able to answer these after 3 minutes:

1. What is Clinica about?
2. What does Patient Stability mean?
3. What does Disease Corruption mean?
4. Why do clues matter?
5. Why should I scout before treating?
6. What happens when I win?
7. What did I restore?
8. What reward did I earn?
9. Why would I play another battle?

Also test:

1. Opening hook appears before first battle.
2. Mission briefing appears before Air Sprite.
3. Battle objective strip is visible.
4. Nonmedical feedback is simple and helpful.
5. Victory screen explains what improved.
6. Air Region progress increases after victory.
7. Kingdom screen shows Air progress.
8. Player sees next mission or next goal.
9. The mobile battle layout still fits on one screen.
10. The notch/dynamic island safe area still works.

Final Design Intent

The game should feel like:

Duolingo-style learning progression
+
Fantasy RPG battles
+
Nursing clinical judgment
+
Body kingdom restoration

The player should not feel like they are just clicking medical buttons.

They should feel like:

I am restoring a living body kingdom by making smarter healing decisions.
