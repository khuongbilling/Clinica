# Step 2: Main Design Rule

Use only two main battle meters:

```text
Patient Stability
Disease Corruption
```

Do not add a third HP stat.

---

## Patient Stability

Represents:

```text
How well the patient is tolerating the situation.
How much safe clinical time the team has.
How close the patient is to collapse.
```

Stability affects:

```text
AP per turn
Treatment effectiveness
Danger event risk
Defeat risk
Star rating
```

---

## Disease Corruption

Represents:

```text
How much control the disease process has over the patient.
```

Disease Corruption is the enemy HP.

Reducing Corruption means the disease process is being controlled.

---

# Step 3: Fix Skill Logic

Main goal:

```text
Every hero should have at least one role-based corruption-lowering skill.
This should not be a generic universal treatment.
The skill should come from the hero’s role and clinical identity.
If the role fits the disease, it should lower Corruption strongly.
If the role does not fit, it should lower Corruption very little.
```

Important:

```text
Do not add a universal weak treatment skill.
Keep Care Attempt as the weak basic fallback.
Care Attempt should remain separate from hero skills.
```

In this codebase, hero skills lower Disease Corruption when they have:

```js
strike: number
```

So the fix is mostly to add or revise `strike` values and clinical metadata.

---

# Step 4: Skill Category Rules

Classify skills by purpose.

---

## Scout

Purpose:

```text
Reveal hidden clue or enemy information.
```

Examples:

```text
Lantern of Clues
Focused Lens
Storm Check
Codex Lab Sigil
```

Effect:

```text
Reveal clue.
Usually should not reduce Corruption.
```

---

## Stabilize

Purpose:

```text
Restore Patient Stability.
```

Examples:

```text
Guardian’s Touch
Breath of Dawn
River Surge
```

Effect:

```text
Increase Patient Stability.
Usually should not directly defeat disease.
```

Exception:

```text
Some mixed role skills like River Surge may stabilize and lower Corruption if clinically appropriate.
```

---

## Shield / Protect

Purpose:

```text
Prevent deterioration or block complication.
```

Examples:

```text
Vital Ward
Safety Circle
Isolation Seal
Barrier Kit
```

Effect:

```text
Block damage, prevent spread, prevent fall, prevent complication.
```

May reduce Corruption only when it directly fits the disease process, such as infection control against Fire/Spread enemies.

---

## Treatment / Counter

Purpose:

```text
Treat the disease cause.
```

Examples:

```text
Airway Rune
Bronchodilator Mist
Sugar Spark
Purity Mark
River Bolus
Splint Ward
Care Plan Ward
Precision Insight
```

Effect:

```text
Reduce Disease Corruption.
```

---

## Reassess

Purpose:

```text
Confirm response after intervention and prevent rebound.
```

Examples:

```text
Reassess
Threadwatch
Codex Link
```

Effect:

```text
Prevent rebound.
Complete care chain.
May give small Stability bonus after correct treatment.
Usually should not directly reduce Corruption unless part of a chain bonus.
```

---

## Command / Consult

Purpose:

```text
Coordinate outside support, prepare items, reduce AP cost, unlock temporary actions.
```

Examples:

```text
Call Pharmacy
Call Respiratory Support
Call Rapid Response
Call Infection Control
Rally Bell
Command Shift
```

Effect:

```text
Supportive effect.
Usually should not directly reduce Corruption.
```

---

# Step 5: Care Attempt Rule

Care Attempt should stay as the basic RPG fallback.

It should:

```text
Cost 1 AP
Consume selected hero action
Reduce Corruption only slightly
Not advance care chain
Not count as correct treatment
Not replace hero role skills
```

Expected balance:

```text
Care Attempt: -5 Corruption
Correct role-based treatment: -25 to -45 Corruption
```

Star rule:

```text
Using Care Attempt more than 2 times should prevent 3-star clear.
Player can still win, but less efficiently.
```

Do not rename Care Attempt to treatment.

Avoid names like:

```text
Basic Treatment
General Treatment
Universal Treatment
```

Care Attempt means unfocused aid, not correct clinical care.

# Step 8: Clinical Fit and Wrong Skills

Wrong role-based skills should not be equally effective.

Example:

```text
Airway Rune vs Air Sprite = strong.
Airway Rune vs Fire Imp = weak or poor fit.
Purity Mark vs Fire Imp = strong.
Purity Mark vs Air Sprite = weak or poor fit.
River Surge vs River Sludge = strong.
River Surge vs fluid overload/heart failure clues = unsafe or poor fit.
Sugar Spark without glucose clue = locked or weak.
Care Plan Ward vs fall/infection/safety = useful.
Care Plan Ward vs pure asthma = weak.
```

Use existing clinical appropriateness and system matching to reduce bad-fit skills.

# Step 9: Pre-Battle Team Choice Should Matter

Do not solve every battle with generic skills.

Players should be encouraged to choose the right team before battle.

Before a battle, show preview information such as:

```text
Enemy: Air Sprite Corruption
Likely System: Air
Visible Clues:
- Breathing Fast
- O2 Glow Dim
- Tripod Position

Recommended Roles:
- Scout
- Air support
- Stabilizer
```

The player should not be hard-locked if they bring the wrong team, but they should likely take more turns and earn fewer stars.

# Step 10: Star and Turn Balance

Use stars to reward efficient clinical care.

## Star 1

```text
Win the battle.
```

## Star 2

```text
Complete the clinical care chain.
```

## Star 3

```text
Win within target turns with no unsafe actions and no excessive Care Attempt use.
```

Care Attempt rule:

```text
More than 2 Care Attempts prevents 3-star clear.
```

Example target turns:

```text
Chapter 1 normal enemy: 4 turns for 3 stars
Chapter 1 boss: 7 turns for 3 stars
Chapter 2 normal enemy: 5 turns for 3 stars
```
